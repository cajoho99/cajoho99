“The Sun(Kerbol) is the most well-known object in the daytime sky. Scientists have noted a particular burning sensation and potential loss of vision if it is stared at for long periods of time. This is especially important to keep in mind considering the effect shiny objects have on the average Kerbal.”

The Sun (also known as Kerbol) is the parent star of the planetary system in KSP, popularly referred to as the Kerbol System. As of version 1.0.5, it has seven known children: the planets Moho, Eve, Kerbin, Duna and Jool and the two dwarf planets Dres and Eeloo.

“Kerbol” is a fan-made portmanteau of “Kerbal” and “Sol” — the Latin name for the real-life Sun. Before version 0.11, which introduced an object for KSP's parent star and an orbital Map view where it was explicitly named, it was an unnamed directional light source at infinite distance. Fans on the KSP forums invented and popularized the name, and the term "Kerbol System" was used in the official 1.2 release post. It is however extremely important to note that the celestial body is called, both in the game and the game files, "Sun." There is no such thing as Kerbol outside of the community forums and wiki.
